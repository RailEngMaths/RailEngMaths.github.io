Session 19: Complex numbers - Cartesian form

This session introduces the imaginary numbers and one form (the Cartesian or rectangular form) of representing the complex numbers. We then learn how basic arithmetic operations can be resolved for such numbers.

Real applications of complex numbers:

In this session we introduce a set of numbers that explicitly do not correspond to any observable physical concept in the real world. Yet the both exist in a mathematical sense, and turn out to have very real applications that can allow us to solve some real problems that we could not address otherwise. One particular area of engineering where complex numbers are used is the design and analysis of electrical circuits. See here for some discussion of this.
